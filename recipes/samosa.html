Samosa


Samosas are a popular crispy and savory snack filled with spiced potatoes, peas, or sometimes meat. Here’s a step-by-step guide to make delicious potato samosas:

Ingredients:

For the Dough(Pastry):
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil or melted ghee
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup water (adjust as needed)
For the Filling:
  • 4 medium potatoes (boiled and mashed)
  • 1/2 cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 1 green chili (finely chopped, optional)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped, optional)
For Frying:
  • Veetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dough:
    1. In a bowl, mix the flour, salt, and oil until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
    2. Gradually add water and knead into a firm but pliable dough. Cover and set aside for 30 minutes.
  2. Make the Filling:
    1. Heat 1 tbsp of oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
    2. Add ginger and green chili, sauté for a few seconds.
    3. Add peas and cook for 2 minutes.
    4. Add turmeric, garam masala, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
    5. Add mashed potatoes and combine everything. Taste and adjust seasoning.
    6. Let the mixture cool, then add chopped coriander leaves if using.
  3. Assemble the Samosas:
    1. Divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls. Roll each ball into a thin oval shape.
    2. Cut the oval in half to make two semicircles.
    3. Take one semicircle, fold it into a cone shape, and seal the edge with water.
    4. Fill the cone with 1–2 tbsp of the potato filling. Seal the top edges by pinching them together with water.
  4. Fry the Samosas:
    1. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat.
    2. Gently add the samosas and fry on medium-low heat until golden brown and crispy. Avoid frying on high heat to ensure they cook evenly.
    3. Drain on a paper towel.
  5. Serve
    Serve hot with mint chutney, tamarind chutney, or ketchup.
